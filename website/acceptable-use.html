Safety

Acceptable Use Policy

Running a model locally does not remove legal or ethical responsibilities. The Store release keeps its local Safety Lock enabled.

Effective July 14, 2026Version 1.1

1. Core principles

Use Local Model Lab in ways that are lawful, consensual, transparent where required, and respectful of the safety and rights of others. You remain responsible for prompts, imported files, model selection, output, and distribution.

2. Prohibited use

  • Sexual abuse or exploitation of minors, grooming, child sexual abuse material, or sexualized depictions of anyone presented as underage.
  • Non-consensual sexual content, sexual violence created for gratification, trafficking, coercion, or intimate imagery created or shared without consent.
  • Credible threats, targeted harassment, stalking, doxxing, violent wrongdoing, or instructions intended to facilitate serious physical harm.
  • Fraud, scams, identity theft, impersonation intended to deceive, forged evidence, or deceptive synthetic media presented as authentic.
  • Malware, credential theft, unauthorized access, destructive cyber activity, persistence, evasion, or data exfiltration intended to harm others.
  • Unlawful discrimination, exploitation of vulnerable people, or denial of rights and services.
  • Infringement of copyright, privacy, publicity, trademark, confidentiality, contractual, or other legal rights.
  • Any use prohibited by the selected model's license or acceptable-use terms.

3. Sexual content and consent

The Store release blocks common sexually explicit prompts. Sexual content involving minors, anyone presented as underage, non-consensual content, and sexual exploitation are always prohibited.

The Safety Lock is always enabled in the Store release and does not grant permission under any law or model license.

4. Cybersecurity use

Defensive learning, authorized testing, vulnerability remediation, and high-level security education are allowed. Do not use Local Model Lab to target systems without authorization, steal credentials, deploy malware, evade detection for abuse, disrupt services, or conceal harmful access.

5. High-impact and professional decisions

Do not use unverified model output as the sole basis for decisions affecting a person's medical treatment, legal rights, liberty, employment, housing, education, insurance, credit, essential services, or access to public benefits. Local Model Lab is not a medical device, law firm, financial adviser, or certified decision system.

6. Identity, privacy, and intellectual property

Obtain necessary consent before using a real person's image, voice, likeness, private information, or confidential material. Clearly disclose synthetic or altered media where context or law requires it. Do not remove provenance information to facilitate deception.

7. Safety controls and limitations

The always-on local prompt guard is a limited keyword and pattern-based control. It can miss harmful requests and may occasionally block benign ones. Model behavior varies. Supervise younger users, review output, and apply stricter controls where your context requires them.

8. Reporting concerns

Use Report unsafe output inside the app for offensive generated content. Report security defects, broken catalog links, license conflicts, or policy issues through the same official GitHub issue tracker. Do not upload illegal content, generated content, private prompts, personal data, credentials, or sensitive paths to a public issue. Catalog entries may be removed while a report is reviewed.
